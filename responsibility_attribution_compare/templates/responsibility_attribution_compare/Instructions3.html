Tournament results: We are also going to mark each gymnast's histogram with the score they received in today's competition. Today's score will show up as a bright red dot at the top of a stack. There will be a red arrow pointing to the dot, so it's easy for you to spot.

Here is an example of how we'd show you scores received by the two gymnasts from before, Smith and Jarrod.

Smith's score today: 9 points

Jarrod's score today: 3 points

We can see that Smith scored 9 points today, marked by a red dot on Smith's heat map above the number 9. Smith gets to move on to the next round of today's competition, because a score of 9 surpasses the 5 point threshold for success. We can also see that Jarrod scored 3 points today, marked by a red dot above the number 3. Jarrod does not get to move on to the next round, because a score of 3 misses the threshold of 5 points.

Your task: We are going to show you the gymnasts' results two at a time. Each pair of gymnasts you'll see are similar in some ways, but different in others. For example, you might see two gymnasts with the same performance history (both earned similar scores in past competitions), but with different scores for today (one might earn 9 points like Smith, while the other earns 7 points, or even 3 points like Jarrod). We'd like you to think about how much responsibility (credit or blame) each gymnast in the pair deserves for his or her individual performance. You will be asked to indicate which gymnast in each pair deserves more credit or blame, or whether you think they each deserve an equal amount.

Overall, you will see the past performance history, and today's competition outcomes, for 48 pairs of gymnasts. For each pair of gymnasts you will use a sliding scale to indicate whether the gymnasts each deserve the same amount, more, or less credit or blame for their performances in the tournament.

After you've seen all 48 pairs, we'll ask you a few very short follow up questions, and that will conclude the HIT.
