Welcome to The Sales Game! In this game, you will play the role of a Technical Sales Representative. Your job is to sell business software platforms. These software platforms support key business processes, like recruiting new employees, managing payroll, and tracking inventory.

The Sales Game has 3 phases. In each phase, you will work for a different company that makes and sells business software platforms. Each company sells 20 software platforms, and each platform supports a separate business process. Each company caters toward a different type of customer, so the software platforms each company makes are unique. In other words, the three companies don't compete against each other - each company's platforms are specialized toward a different set of customer needs.

You will work for each company for 15 rounds, and then you will move on and work for a different company. On each round, you will make a pitch to a new customer. You will make 15 pitches for each of the three companies (45 pitches total, 45 rounds total).

You can only include 3 of a company's 20 software platforms in each pitch you make to one of their customers. To maximize your chances of making a sale, you need to figure out which 3 platforms each company's customers value the most.

After you submit a pitch, the customer will let you know how much they value the platforms in that pitch by assigning points to each platform. Each company's customers have similar business needs, so they tend to agree about which of the company's platforms are more valuable than others. For example, if Platform X is the most valuable platform that a company makes, every customer will assign a high point value to Platform X. The exact point value each customer assigns to Platform X might differ by a few points, but you'll notice that, on average, customers assign higher point values to Platform X than to other platforms.

Each round, you can choose to pitch the same 3 platforms you chose in the previous round, or you can swap out any or all of those platforms for different ones. At the end of each round, we will add together the points the customer assigns to each platform in your pitch to come up with your Total Pitch Score for that round.

Your Total Pitch Scores will determine the size of the bonus payment you receive. At the end of the game, we will add up the Total Pitch Scores you earned each round to determine your bonus. You will earn a bonus of 1 cent for every 100 game points (example: 5000 game points = $0.50 bonus). On average, people earn a bonus of about $6.00.
